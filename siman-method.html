The Siman
Method™

A structured framework designed to improve communication, coordination, documentation, and execution in property maintenance and remodeling projects.

Work With Us

The Siman Method™ is a structured operational framework developed through hands-on experience coordinating maintenance operations, remodeling projects, service requests, and property workflows across South Florida communities.

The framework was designed to improve communication, documentation, coordination, scheduling visibility, and operational accountability in environments where multiple residents, vendors, approvals, and urgent situations must be managed efficiently.

Origin

Why It Was Developed

Recurring gaps identified through years of field operations.

Delayed Communication

Property managers and residents were left without timely updates — creating frustration and eroding trust during active projects.

Lack of Documentation

Work was completed without photos, notes, or records — making it impossible to verify quality or trace what was done.

Inconsistent Follow-Through

Jobs started but weren't tracked to completion — leading to unresolved issues and repeat visits.

Poor Operational Visibility

No clear view of what was pending, approved, in progress, or completed — across properties, buildings, and units.

The Framework

Six Phases

From diagnosis to delivery — every step documented.

01

Issue Detection & Intake

Every request is received, logged, and categorized — whether it comes from a resident, manager, or field inspection. Nothing falls through the cracks.

02

Priority Classification

Issues are assessed by urgency, impact, and scope. Emergency requests are escalated immediately; scheduled work is organized by building and priority level.

03

Work Order Generation

A documented work order is created with scope, timeline, assigned team, and approval status — before any work begins.

04

Live Field Execution

Teams execute with real-time updates. Photos are captured throughout. Progress is tracked and visible to property managers as it happens.

05

Quality Verification

Every completed job is reviewed against the original scope before closure. No shortcuts, no assumptions — verification is built into the process.

06

Documentation & Closure

Final photos, notes, and records are saved and shared. The property manager receives a complete closure summary for every work order.
